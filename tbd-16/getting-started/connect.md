# Connect & Power

Before anything else, wire up the **tbd 16** so you can hear it and (optionally) connect it to a computer.
{: .fs-6 .fw-300 }

![tbd 16 — top view](../../../images/tbd-16/dadamachines-tbd16-drawing.png)

---

## 1. Power

Plug a USB-C cable into **USB-C port #1** on the back of the device. Any standard USB-C power supply, USB battery pack, or computer USB port works.

The same port also carries USB MIDI and the USB network connection — so one cable to your computer powers the device **and** exposes its WebUI and MIDI endpoints.

---

## 2. Audio output

You have two options for listening:

| Output | Where | Use for |
|:-------|:------|:--------|
| **Line Out** | Back of the device — stereo TRS 3.5 mm | Monitors, mixer, audio interface, external recorder |
| **Headphone Out** | Front of the device — stereo TRS 3.5 mm | Headphones. Volume is set with the dedicated headphone volume wheel next to the jack. |
{: .dada-minimal-table }

Both outputs are active simultaneously.

---

## 3. Connecting to a computer (optional)

A single USB-C cable from **USB-C #1** to your computer gives you:

- **USB MIDI** — the **tbd 16** shows up as a MIDI device in your DAW
- **USB Network (NCM)** — open the WebUI in a browser at the address shown on the device under **FUNC1 → Settings → WIFI Settings** (no WiFi required)

See [WiFi Settings](../project-and-settings/wifi-settings) to pick between USB Network mode and Access Point mode.
