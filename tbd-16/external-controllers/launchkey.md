# Novation Launchkey

Plug a **Launchkey Mk4** into the tbd 16 and you have a keyboard, 16 velocity pads, 8 knobs and transport controls — all mapped to the Groovebox app. The controller switches into the right mode automatically and its little display shows the active track and preset.
{: .fs-6 .fw-300 }

---

## Supported models

- Launchkey **Mini Mk4** (25-key)
- Launchkey **Mk4** — 25, 37, 49 and 61-key

Plug in, the keyboard and pads light up, you're ready.

---

## Keys, wheels & sustain

Play keys and the active track responds with velocity. The pitch-bend wheel, mod wheel and sustain pedal all work as normal. The Launchkey's built-in **arpeggiator** runs on the controller itself, so you can drive any track on the tbd 16 with on-device arp patterns.

{: .tip }
> Hold a chord on the keys and spin the encoders — you're playing and tweaking the sound at the same time.

---

## 16 pads — step entry

The 2×8 pad grid is the 16 steps of the current page on the active track:

- Top row → steps **1–8**
- Bottom row → steps **9–16**

Tap a pad to toggle that step on or off. Use encoder 5 (below) to move between the four step pages.

---

## 8 encoders

The encoders are set to infinite-rotation mode so they scroll without hitting ends:

| Encoder | What it controls |
|:-------:|:-----------------|
| 1 – 4 | **Knobs 1–4** of the current parameter page — same as the four hardware knobs on the tbd 16 |
| 5 | Step page — scroll between pages 1–4 |
| 6 | Track select — previous / next track |
| 7 – 8 | Reserved for future updates |
{: .dada-minimal-table }

The **Plugin / Mixer / Sends / Transport** mode buttons above the encoders are accepted and reserved for additional layouts in a future firmware.

---

## Transport & navigation

| Button | What it does |
|:-------|:-------------|
| Play | Play / pause |
| Stop | Play / pause (dedicated stop behaviour coming later) |
| Record | Arm / toggle record |
| ▲ / ▼ | Up / down in menus and parameter pages |
| Track ◄ / ► | Previous / next track |
| ► (right arrow) | Cycle the step page forward |
{: .dada-minimal-table }

---

## Display

The tbd 16 writes the **track name** and **preset name** to the Launchkey's small display over USB, so you can keep your eyes on the controller while performing and still know what you're playing.

---

## Using Launchpad + Launchkey together

Through a self-powered USB hub you can plug in both controllers at the same time:

- **Launchpad** — grid duties: steps, drums, pattern launch, chromatic play.
- **Launchkey** — keys, wheels, transport and four knobs.

Either one is hot-pluggable — the other keeps working.
